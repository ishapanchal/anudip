China to launch at least two manned missions to space every year

China completed the construction of its Tiangong space station and is looking to enhance scientific experiments at the zero gravity lab.

China has said it will conduct two manned and two cargo mission every year.:

Moving fast in its ambitious plans beyond Earth, China has announced that it will launch at least two manned missions to its newly constructed Tiangong space station every year. The China Manned Space Agency (CMSA) has said that apart from two manned missions, two cargo spacecraft will also take off with resupplies to the manned outpost.The Chinese space agency is already in the midst of prepping for the Tianzhou-6 cargo ship launch in May, which will dock with the microgravity lab. The ship will be attached to the aft port of the core module, forming a configuration of three modules and two ships on the laboratory.
